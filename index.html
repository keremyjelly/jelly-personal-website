About

Hello! I'm Jeremy, a sophomore at the University of Notre Dame studying Computer Science. I'm also on the Men's Swim Team specializing in sprint freestyle and IM, and I'm a resident of Carroll Hall (AAMW).


I grew up in Georgetown, Texas with two older brothers and three dogs. I started swimming when I was four years old, and it's been a central part of my life ever since. I attended Georgetown High School and swam for Nitro Swimming.


I initially attended the University of Texas at Austin. During my first year at UT, I studied aerospace engineering while competing on the swim team. Our team won both the SEC Championship and the NCAA National Championship that season. Later, changes in the NCAA landscape led me to transfer to Notre Dame, where I shifted my academic focus to computer science. Best decision ever.


I'm very interested in machine learning, and I want to get under the hood to understand the mechanics, math, and systems behind the magic. Teaching and mentoring are also passions of mine. I love learning and building things, and I want to help other people do the same. In my opinion, seeing someone succeed because of guidance or support you gave them is one of the most fulfilling experiences.


In terms of work experience, I am a cofounder of the nonprofit organization Ten at a Time, which I remained involved with for over ten years. I also worked as a cave tour guide.


Outside of school, I spend most of my time with friends and family, exploring nature, hiking, and traveling whenever I get the chance. I'm also a huge fan of all things movies, music, anime, and sci-fi / fantasy books. I also love good food (especially good bread), but who doesn't.
